Overview

Desertflow is a noncommercial research project building a per-region NDVI prediction system to study desertification mechanisms across 133 ecoregions worldwide. The framework combines region-specific CatBoost specialists with a per-cell Bayesian-ridge router (B2 architecture) to predict vegetation response to climate, soil, fire, and anthropogenic stressors.

Project Details

  • Scope: 133 WWF terrestrial ecoregions, 2002–2024 (22 years), monthly cadence.
  • Drivers: 19 Earth Engine modules covering MODIS NDVI/LST, CHIRPS precipitation, ERA5 climate, SMAP soil moisture, GLDAS, fire activity, snow phenology, VPD extremes, and topographic context.
  • Modeling: per-region CatBoost specialists combined with a per-cell Bayesian-ridge router, trained with explicit temporal-leakage audits.
  • Validation: held-out 44-region cross-continental test set; leakage-safe driver list with no NDVI-derived inputs in 2030-prediction-safe models.
Headline result: cross-continental mean R² ≈ 0.93 on held-out test regions after rigorous leakage controls — comparable to within-region baselines that typically benefit from data leakage.

Methodological Contribution

The distinguishing contribution of this work is a leakage-aware validation protocol. Many published NDVI forecasting studies inadvertently leak future information through:

  • Sister vegetation features (NDVI-derived land cover, GPP, phenology metrics)
  • Shared cross-region statistics in scalers or imputers
  • Improperly designed cross-validation splits
  • Pheno-prior features computed from the target NDVI series itself

Bundle Research documents and tests against each failure mode before reporting metrics.

This work requires sustained Earth Engine compute for large-scale ecoregion processing — approximately 30,000 batch export tasks per full bundle refresh. Registered under Google Earth Engine's noncommercial research program (project ID: celtic-science-493107-c6).
