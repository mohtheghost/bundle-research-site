Bundle Research

Independent Earth observation research from Riyadh, Saudi Arabia. Per-region NDVI forecasting across 133 WWF ecoregions using Google Earth Engine.

About

I am an independent high school student researcher based in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia. "Bundle Research" is the name I use for my Earth observation work, focused on desertification and vegetation dynamics in arid and semi-arid regions. The work supports UN Convention to Combat Desertification (UNCCD) goals and Sustainable Development Goal 15.3 (Land Degradation Neutrality).

Active Project — Desertflow Global Bundle

  • Scope: 133 WWF terrestrial ecoregions, 2002–2024 (22 years), monthly cadence.
  • Drivers: 19 Earth Engine modules covering MODIS NDVI/LST, CHIRPS precipitation, ERA5 climate, SMAP soil moisture, GLDAS, fire activity, snow phenology, VPD extremes, and topographic context.
  • Modeling: per-region CatBoost specialists combined with a per-cell Bayesian-ridge router architecture, trained with explicit temporal-leakage audits.
  • Headline result: cross-continental mean R² ≈ 0.93 on held-out test regions after leakage controls.

Methods

The project's distinguishing methodological contribution is a leakage-aware validation protocol. Many published NDVI forecasting studies inadvertently leak future information through sister vegetation features (NDVI-derived land-cover, GPP, phenology metrics), shared cross-region statistics, or improperly designed cross-validation splits. Bundle Research documents and tests against each of these failure modes before reporting metrics. The validation harness, driver modules, and trained models will be released publicly upon publication.

Earth Engine Compute

This work requires sustained Earth Engine compute for large-scale ecoregion processing — approximately 30,000 batch export tasks per full bundle refresh across 133 regions × 22 years × 19 driver modules. The project is registered under Google Earth Engine's noncommercial research program.
